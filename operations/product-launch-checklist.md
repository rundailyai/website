# Product Launch Checklist SOP

## Overview
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) outlines all steps required when launching a new digital product on RunDaily.ai.

## Pre-Launch Phase (T-7 Days)

### Product Preparation
- [ ] Product content finalized and QA tested
- [ ] Product files formatted and optimized
- [ ] Product title and description written
- [ ] Pricing strategy confirmed
- [ ] Product category/tags defined
- [ ] Legal review completed (if needed)

### Marketing Assets
- [ ] Product thumbnail created (1200x630px)
- [ ] Preview images prepared (2-4 images)
- [ ] Marketing copy written
- [ ] Value proposition clearly defined
- [ ] Target audience identified
- [ ] Key benefits/features listed

### Technical Setup
- [ ] Product uploaded to Polar store
- [ ] Product page configured (draft mode)
- [ ] Download links tested
- [ ] Payment flow verified
- [ ] Mobile responsiveness checked
- [ ] SEO metadata added

### Marketing Preparation
- [ ] Social media posts drafted (Twitter, LinkedIn)
- [ ] Email announcement drafted
- [ ] Launch timing scheduled
- [ ] Hashtags and keywords researched
- [ ] Influencer/partner outreach list prepared (if applicable)

## Launch Day (T-0)

### Morning (8:00 AM)
- [ ] Final product quality check
- [ ] Verify all links and downloads work
- [ ] Set product status to "Published" on Polar
- [ ] Verify product appears on store page
- [ ] Test purchase flow one final time

### Launch Announcement (9:00 AM)
- [ ] Publish product page on rundaily.ai
- [ ] Send launch email to subscriber list
- [ ] Post on Twitter with product link
- [ ] Post on LinkedIn with product link
- [ ] Post on relevant Reddit communities (if applicable)
- [ ] Share in relevant Slack/Discord communities
- [ ] Update website homepage with new product (if featured)

### Monitoring (Throughout Day)
- [ ] Monitor social media mentions
- [ ] Respond to comments and questions within 1 hour
- [ ] Track sales/downloads in real-time
- [ ] Monitor customer support email
- [ ] Check for any technical issues
- [ ] Engage with early customers

### Evening Review (6:00 PM)
- [ ] Review first-day sales numbers
- [ ] Compile customer feedback
- [ ] Identify any issues or concerns
- [ ] Adjust marketing copy if needed
- [ ] Plan next day's follow-up posts

## Post-Launch Phase

### Week 1
- [ ] Daily monitoring of sales and feedback
- [ ] Respond to all customer inquiries within 24 hours
- [ ] Post 2-3 follow-up social media updates
- [ ] Share customer testimonials (if received)
- [ ] Address any bugs or issues immediately
- [ ] Update product description based on FAQ patterns

### Week 2-4
- [ ] Weekly sales review
- [ ] Compile and analyze customer reviews
- [ ] Create case studies from successful customers
- [ ] Produce additional marketing content
- [ ] Consider running a promotion (if sales are slow)
- [ ] Update product based on feedback

### Ongoing
- [ ] Monthly performance review
- [ ] Quarterly product updates (if needed)
- [ ] Maintain customer support response times
- [ ] Monitor competitor products
- [ ] Collect testimonials and case studies

## Success Metrics

Track these KPIs for the first 30 days:
- Total sales/downloads
- Conversion rate (page views → purchases)
- Customer support tickets
- Customer satisfaction rating
- Social media engagement
- Email open and click rates
- Refund rate
- Customer reviews/testimonials

## Launch Issue Escalation

### Critical Issues (Immediate Action)
- Product files not downloading
- Payment processing errors
- Website/store page down
- Major bug affecting functionality

**Action**: Fix immediately, notify all customers if affected

### High Priority Issues (Same Day)
- Customer complaints about quality
- Multiple refund requests
- Negative reviews
- Marketing copy errors

**Action**: Assess and resolve within 4-8 hours

### Medium Priority Issues (24-48 Hours)
- Feature requests
- Minor bugs
- Marketing optimization
- SEO improvements

**Action**: Log and plan resolution

## Team Responsibilities

### Pre-Launch
- **COO/Ops**: Checklist management, coordination
- **Product Creator**: Final product QA
- **Marketing**: Assets and copy
- **Tech**: Store setup and testing

### Launch Day
- **COO/Ops**: Launch execution, monitoring
- **Marketing**: Social media posting
- **Support**: Customer inquiries
- **Tech**: Issue monitoring and fixes

### Post-Launch
- **COO/Ops**: Performance review, feedback analysis
- **Product**: Updates and improvements
- **Marketing**: Ongoing promotion
- **Support**: Customer satisfaction

## Notes & Best Practices

1. **Launch Timing**: Tuesday-Thursday, 9 AM EST typically performs best
2. **Launch Offers**: Consider 20% off for first 48 hours to drive early sales
3. **Social Proof**: Collect testimonials from beta testers before launch
4. **Email List**: Warm up subscribers with teaser emails 2-3 days before
5. **Community**: Engage with early adopters to create momentum
6. **Follow-up**: Plan 2-3 follow-up marketing pushes in the weeks after launch
