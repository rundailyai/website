# RunDailyAI Brand Guidelines

## Brand Identity

**Mission**: Empower professionals to stay ahead with AI-curated insights delivered daily.

**Vision**: Be the trusted daily companion for AI-aware professionals navigating the rapidly evolving landscape of artificial intelligence.

**Target Audience**: Knowledge workers, tech professionals, executives, and curious learners who want to stay informed about AI without information overload.

---

## Brand Voice

### Core Voice Attributes

**Tone**: Intelligent, approachable, confident, and conversational

**Personality**:
- Curious and forward-thinking
- Credible but not academic
- Helpful without being patronizing
- Energetic but not hype-driven
- Clear and concise

### Voice Guidelines by Context

**Website**
- ✅ DO: Use clear, benefit-focused language
- ✅ DO: Speak directly to reader pain points (information overload, staying current)
- ✅ DO: Balance authority with accessibility
- ❌ DON'T: Use buzzwords without substance ("revolutionize", "disrupt")
- ❌ DON'T: Make promises we can't keep
- ❌ DON'T: Use technical jargon without context

**Blog**
- ✅ DO: Lead with insights and takeaways
- ✅ DO: Use data and examples to support claims
- ✅ DO: Write in first person plural ("we noticed", "our analysis")
- ❌ DON'T: Editorialize without evidence
- ❌ DON'T: Chase clickbait headlines
- ❌ DON'T: Ignore nuance for simplicity

**Email**
- ✅ DO: Get to the point quickly
- ✅ DO: Use scannable formatting (bullets, short paragraphs)
- ✅ DO: Create urgency without being salesy
- ❌ DON'T: Bury the lede
- ❌ DON'T: Over-promise features
- ❌ DON'T: Use ALL CAPS or excessive punctuation!!!

**Social Media**
- ✅ DO: Lead with hooks that spark curiosity
- ✅ DO: Use questions to engage
- ✅ DO: Share specific insights, not generic wisdom
- ❌ DON'T: Post without a clear point
- ❌ DON'T: Engage in controversy for attention
- ❌ DON'T: Sound like a corporate robot

## Typography

### Font Families

**Headings**: **Inter** (Google Fonts)
- Weights: 600 (Semi-Bold), 700 (Bold), 800 (Extra Bold)
- Modern, clean, highly readable at all sizes
- Alternative: Archivo, Work Sans

**Body Text**: **Inter** (Google Fonts)
- Weights: 400 (Regular), 500 (Medium), 600 (Semi-Bold)
- Excellent readability for long-form content
- Alternative: System fonts (SF Pro, Segoe UI)

### Type Scale

**Hero/H1**: 48px/56px (desktop), 36px/44px (mobile)
**H2**: 36px/44px (desktop), 28px/36px (mobile)
**H3**: 28px/36px (desktop), 24px/32px (mobile)
**H4**: 24px/32px
**H5**: 20px/28px
**Body Large**: 18px/28px
**Body**: 16px/24px
**Body Small**: 14px/20px
**Caption**: 12px/16px

### Typography Guidelines

- Line height: 1.5–1.75 for body text
- Line length: 60–80 characters max for readability
- Letter spacing: -0.01em for headings, default for body
- Use sentence case for most headings (not Title Case)

## Platform-Specific Tone Guides

### Website
**Tone**: Professional, confident, clear
**Language**: Benefit-driven, solution-focused
**Length**: Concise but comprehensive
**CTA Style**: Direct and action-oriented ("Start your daily brief", "See what's new in AI")

### X (Twitter)
**Tone**: Sharp, insightful, conversational
**Language**: Punchy and quotable
**Length**: 200-280 characters ideal
**Style**: Lead with the insight, use line breaks for readability
**Example**: "AI models aren't getting smarter by accident.\n\nThey're getting better training data.\n\nHere's what changed this week:"

### LinkedIn
**Tone**: Professional, thought-leadership oriented
**Language**: Industry-focused, data-backed
**Length**: 150-300 words for posts, shorter for comments
**Style**: Start with a hook, support with data/examples, end with a question or CTA
**Example**: "3 trends we're seeing in enterprise AI adoption this quarter... [insights] What are you seeing in your organization?"

### Instagram
**Tone**: Visual, engaging, accessible
**Language**: Simple and impactful
**Length**: 125-150 characters optimal
**Style**: Lead with emotion/benefit, use emojis sparingly (1-3 max)
**Example**: "Your AI news digest just got smarter. No fluff. Just insights. Daily. 🚀"

### Pinterest
**Tone**: Inspirational, educational
**Language**: How-to and benefit-focused
**Length**: Short descriptions (50-100 chars)
**Style**: Pin infographics, visual guides, article highlights
**Example**: "5 AI Tools Every Professional Should Know | RunDailyAI"

### TikTok
**Tone**: Energetic, educational, relatable
**Language**: Conversational, trending format awareness
**Length**: 60-90 second videos
**Style**: Quick hooks, fast-paced information, clear takeaways
**Example**: "POV: You're trying to keep up with AI news [relatable struggle] → Here's how we solve it [solution]"

### Reddit
**Tone**: Authentic, community-oriented, helpful
**Language**: Conversational, humble, value-first
**Length**: Varies by subreddit
**Style**: Never self-promote without providing real value first; focus on answering questions and participating genuinely
**Example**: "I've been tracking AI tools for my team. Here's what actually worked... [honest insights]"

## Key Messaging

### What We Say

**Core Message**: "RunDailyAI curates the signal from the noise in AI, delivering what matters to your inbox every day."

**Supporting Messages**:
- "Stay informed without information overload"
- "AI insights curated by experts, delivered to you"
- "Your competitive advantage in the age of AI"
- "From breakthrough research to practical tools"
- "Understanding AI doesn't require a PhD"

### How We Say It

**We are**:
- Clear and direct
- Evidence-based and credible
- Helpful and empowering
- Honest about limitations
- Future-focused but grounded

**We use**:
- Active voice
- "You" to address readers directly
- Specific examples over generic claims
- Data to support insights
- Questions to engage thinking

### What We Never Say

❌ "AI will replace humans" (doom narratives)
❌ "This will revolutionize everything" (empty hype)
❌ "You NEED to act NOW or miss out" (false urgency/FOMO)
❌ "It's so simple anyone can do it" (dismissive of real complexity)
❌ "Trust us, we're experts" (appeal to authority without proof)
❌ "AI-powered" without explaining what that actually means

**Our Stance on AI Ethics**:
- Acknowledge both opportunities and risks
- Support responsible AI development
- Advocate for transparency and human agency
- Avoid taking political stances unrelated to AI

## Content Principles

### 1. **Clarity First**
Complex AI concepts should be explained in simple terms without losing accuracy.

### 2. **Curation Over Volume**
We filter signal from noise—quality and relevance over quantity.

### 3. **Actionable Insights**
Every piece of content should help readers understand, decide, or act.

### 4. **Evidence-Based**
Claims are backed by data, research, or credible sources.

### 5. **Respectful of Time**
Get to the point. Respect that our audience is busy.

### 6. **Consistent Value**
Every daily brief, blog post, or update should justify the time investment.

## Visual Identity Notes

### Photography Style
- Clean, modern, high-quality
- Diverse representation of professionals
- Avoid generic stock imagery
- Use illustrations/icons for abstract AI concepts

### Graphic Style
- Minimalist and modern
- Geometric shapes to represent data/AI
- Gradients using brand colors (subtle)
- Clear information hierarchy

### UI/UX Voice
- Button text: action-oriented ("Get Started", "Read Today's Brief")
- Form labels: clear and direct
- Error messages: helpful, not condescending
- Empty states: encouraging, not alarming
