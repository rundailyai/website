# Bonus Email Templates

**10 Additional Templates for Specific Situations**

These bonus templates cover special scenarios that don't fit into standard sequences but are commonly needed for cold outreach.

---

## 1. Re-Engagement After Long Silence (6+ Months)

**Use Case:** Following up with a prospect you talked to 6+ months ago

**Subject:** Circling back - [Company Name]

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

We talked [timeframe] ago about [topic/service], but timing wasn't right.

Circling back because [specific reason - e.g., "I saw Company Name raised Series B" or "noticed you launched [new product]" or "Q1 is typically when companies revisit [topic]"].

Since we last talked:
• [Your company update #1 - new service, client win, etc.]
• [Your company update #2]
• [Relevant market change]

Still dealing with [problem you solve]? Or did you find a solution?

[Your Name]

**When to use:** 6-12 months after last contact, triggered by company news or calendar cycle

**Event:** [Event Name]

**Speakers:** [Notable speaker names/titles]

**What you'll learn:**
• [Takeaway #1]
• [Takeaway #2]
• [Takeaway #3]

Register here: [link]

[Optional: We have [X] spots left for [their industry] companies - register by [date] to secure your place.]

[Your Name]

**When to use:** Promoting events, webinars, workshops; 2-4 weeks before event date

## 3. Content Share (Soft Touch)

**Use Case:** Sharing valuable content without asking for anything

**Subject:** Thought this might interest you - [Topic]

**Body:**

No ask here - just wanted to share something I thought you'd find useful.

We just published [content type] on [topic]: [link]

It covers:
• [Key point #1]
• [Key point #2]
• [Key point #3]

The section on [specific section] is especially relevant for [their company type] companies dealing with [their likely challenge].

[Your Name]

**When to use:** Building relationship, staying top-of-mind, after break-up email, before re-engagement

## 4. Mutual Connection Introduction

**Use Case:** Leveraging mutual connection for warm intro

**Subject:** [Mutual Connection] suggested I reach out

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

[Mutual Connection Name] suggested I reach out - they thought [Company Name] might benefit from [what you offer].

Quick context: I help [their company type] companies with [specific problem]. [Mutual Connection] and I [how you know them - worked together, client, etc.].

They mentioned you're [specific situation - expanding, dealing with X, exploring Y]. We recently helped [Similar Company] with [similar situation], achieving [specific outcome].

Worth a brief conversation? Happy to share what worked for [Similar Company] and see if it's relevant to [Company Name].

[Your Name]

P.S. - [Mutual Connection] said to mention [specific thing they told you to reference - inside joke, shared experience, specific context].

**When to use:** When you have a genuine mutual connection who recommended reaching out

## 5. Competitor Mention (Tactful)

**Use Case:** When prospect is using a competitor and you have clear advantage

**Subject:** [Company Name] + [Competitor] - quick question

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I noticed [Company Name] is using [Competitor Product/Service] - [how you know - LinkedIn, website, mutual client mentioned it].

No pitch here, just curious: Are you happy with [Competitor]? We work with a lot of former [Competitor] clients who switched because of [specific pain point Competitor has].

**Common reasons for switching:**
• [Reason #1 - e.g., "Limited integration with X"]
• [Reason #2 - e.g., "Pricing jumps significantly at scale"]
• [Reason #3 - e.g., "Lack of Y feature"]

If you're experiencing any of those, happy to show you how we approach it differently. If you're happy with [Competitor], great - no need to reply.

[Your Name]

**When to use:** When you have clear competitive advantage and evidence prospect uses competitor; use tactfully, not aggressively

## 6. Job Posting Trigger

**Body:**

We work with a lot of companies at this exact stage, helping them [what you do] before/while they scale their [department/process].

**Recent example:**
[Similar Company] was hiring for [similar role]. We helped them [specific outcome] which made their new hire's ramp-up much faster.

Worth a conversation before your new [role] starts? Could save them [specific time/headache].

[Your Name]

**When to use:** When you see relevant job postings on LinkedIn, company career page, or job boards

• [Timing reason #1 - e.g., "Most companies want to start new tools/services in Q1"]

[Your Name]

**When to use:**

- Mid-year: "Second half" planning

## Bonus Template Usage Guidelines

### When to Use These Templates:

**Re-Engagement (Template 1):**
- 6-12 months after last contact
- After major company milestone
- Calendar-based timing (new year, new quarter)

**Event Invitation (Template 2):**
- 2-4 weeks before event
- To prospects in nurture sequence
- To prospects who didn't convert but were engaged

**Content Share (Template 3):**
- After break-up email (stay top of mind)
- Monthly/quarterly nurture
- When you have genuinely valuable content

**Mutual Connection (Template 4):**
- When you have warm intro opportunity
- Higher priority than cold outreach
- Reference mutual connection early in subject line

**Competitor Mention (Template 5):**
- When you have proof they use competitor
- Only if you have clear advantage
- Use tactfully (not trash-talking)

**Job Posting (Template 6):**
- Within 1 week of job posting
- For roles directly related to what you sell
- Shows you're paying attention

**Funding News (Template 7):**
- Within 1-2 weeks of announcement
- Connect funding to predictable needs
- Show you understand post-funding challenges

**Seasonal Hook (Template 8):**
- Q4 for year-end budget conversations
- Q1 for new year initiatives
- Industry-specific timing (tax season, etc.)

**Award/Recognition (Template 9):**
- Within 1 week of announcement
- Genuine congratulations (not fake)
- Connect recognition to relevant service

**LinkedIn Post (Template 10):**
- Same day or next day after they post
- Only if you have relevant expertise to add
- Comment on post AND send email

## Combining Templates with Core Sequences

**Strategic Sequencing:**

**Scenario 1: Saw job posting**
1. Send Job Posting email (Bonus #6)
2. If no response, start relevant core sequence 1 week later

**Scenario 2: Funding announcement**
3. Send Funding email (Bonus #7)
4. If no response, send Content Share (Bonus #3) 1 week later
5. If still no response, start core sequence

**Scenario 3: LinkedIn engagement**
- Comment on their LinkedIn post
- Send LinkedIn Post email (Bonus #10) same day
- If they respond, book meeting
- If no response, add to regular core sequence starting 1 week later

**Rule of thumb:** Trigger-based bonus emails can be sent outside normal sequence cadence, but don't spam. Maximum 1 email per week.

---

## Personalization Requirements for Bonus Templates

**All bonus templates require MORE personalization than core sequences:**

- Reference specific, verifiable information (funding amount, award name, job title, LinkedIn post excerpt)
- Connect trigger event to their specific situation
- Add value beyond "I saw this news about you"
- Keep it genuine (no fake congratulations)

**If you can't personalize meaningfully, don't send the bonus template** - use a core sequence instead.

**Bonus Template Performance vs. Core Sequences:**

| Template | Response Rate | Meeting Rate | Notes |
|----------|---------------|--------------|-------|
| Re-Engagement | 8-15% | 3-6% | Higher if major trigger event |

| Competitor Mention | 10-18% | 4-8% | Risky but effective if done tactfully |
| Job Posting | 15-25% | 7-12% | High relevance = high response |

**Highest performers:** Mutual Connection, LinkedIn Post, Funding News, Job Posting

**Use these strategically** - they're most effective when there's a genuine trigger event, not forced.

## A/B Testing Bonus Templates

**Test variables:**

**Timing:**
- Immediate (same day as trigger) vs. 3-day delay
- Morning vs. afternoon send times

**Tone:**
- Casual/friendly vs. professional/formal
- Short (3-4 sentences) vs. medium length

**CTA:**
- Direct calendar link vs. "reply to schedule"
- Specific benefit ("15-min call to discuss X") vs. open-ended

**Value-add:**
- Include resource/content vs. pure outreach
- Mention specific next step vs. exploratory

**Track what works for your industry and adjust accordingly.**
