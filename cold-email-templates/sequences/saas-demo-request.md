# SaaS - Demo Request Sequence

**Industry:** SaaS
**Use Case:** Product Demo / Trial Request
**Sequence Length:** 5 emails over 30 days
**Best For:** Getting prospects to see your product in action

---

## Email 1: Initial Demo Offer (Day 0)

**Subject:** [Company Name] - [Specific Feature] demo

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I help [their industry] companies like [Similar Company 1] and [Similar Company 2] solve [specific problem] with [Your Product].

Most [their role] teams we work with are struggling with [pain point A] and [pain point B]. Here's what we've built to fix that:

**[Feature #1]:** [One sentence on what it does + outcome]
**[Feature #2]:** [One sentence on what it does + outcome]
**[Feature #3]:** [One sentence on what it does + outcome]

Rather than a long explanation, would a 15-minute screen share demo next week make sense? I can show you exactly how [Similar Company] is using [specific feature] to [specific outcome].

You can grab time here: [calendly link]

[Your Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Focus on 3 features that solve their likely pain points
- Use outcomes not features ("reduce time by 40%" not "automated workflows")
- Name similar companies they'd recognize

**Subject:** Re: [Company Name] - [Specific Feature] demo

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Quick follow-up - I know scheduling can be a pain, so I created an interactive demo you can explore on your own time.

[Interactive Demo Link]

This walkthrough shows:
• [Specific use case #1] (3 min)
• [Specific use case #2] (4 min)
• [Specific use case #3] (2 min)

**No signup required** - just click and explore.

If anything resonates and you want to see how it'd work with your specific [workflow/process/tech stack], happy to jump on a 10-minute call to customize it for [Company Name].

[Your Name]

P.S. - The [Specific Feature] section at timestamp 2:15 is what [Similar Company] said made them switch from [Competitor Name].

**Personalization Notes:**
- Use tools like Loom, Vidyard, or Navattic for interactive demos
- Timestamp the most relevant section for their use case
- Mention competitor they're likely using (shows you know their space)

[Specific implementation approach - 1-2 sentences]

**Results in 90 days:**
• [Metric #1]

• [Metric #3]

## Email 4: "Quick Question" Break-Up (Day 10-12)

**Subject:** Quick question

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I've sent over a couple of demos but haven't heard back. Before I move on, quick question:

Is [pain point you solve] not a priority for [Company Name] right now?

Or is it that you're already handling this with [Competitor A] or [Competitor B]?

Either answer helps me - if it's a "not right now" situation, I'll check back in [3/6] months. If it's a "we're good" situation, I'll take you off my list.

[Your Name]

P.S. - If you *are* evaluating tools and just busy, here's our comparison sheet showing us vs [Competitor A/B]: [link]. Might save you research time.

**Personalization Notes:**
- Be direct and respectful
- Show you know their space (mention real competitors)
- The P.S. comparison sheet often gets responses from active buyers

**Why it matters for [Company Name]:**
[Specific benefit #1 related to their likely pain point]
[Specific benefit #2 related to their industry]

Early access demo (only for [their industry] companies right now): [calendly link]

We're onboarding the first 10 [industry] teams with hands-on implementation help - happy to save you a spot if you want to be part of that.

**Personalization Notes:**

- Offer extra support to make it low-risk for them

## Sequence Timing & Optimization

### Best Times to Send Demo-Focused Emails

| Email | Day | Best Send Time | Why |
|-------|-----|---------------|-----|
| Email 1 | Day 0 | Tuesday-Wednesday, 10am-12pm | Midweek, mid-morning = more likely to book meetings |
| Email 2 | Day 3-4 | Same day of week as Email 1, similar time | Pattern recognition + availability |
| Email 3 | Day 7 | Thursday morning | End of week, people planning ahead |
| Email 4 | Day 10-12 | Monday or Friday | Break-ups perform well at week edges |
| Email 5 | Day 21-30 | Tuesday-Wednesday | Fresh start, midweek energy |

---

## Demo Best Practices

### For Live Demos:
1. **Start with their use case** - "Based on our brief chat, sounds like you need to [X]"
2. **Show outcome first, features second** - Demo backwards from result
3. **Keep it to 15 minutes max** - Respect their time
4. **End with clear next step** - "Does this solve [pain]? If yes, I can get you a trial today."

### For Recorded Demos:
1. **Keep under 5 minutes** - Attention span is limited
2. **Add timestamps** - Let them skip to relevant parts
3. **Show real data** - Not "example" or "test" accounts
4. **Include voiceover** - Don't make them read text

## Expected Results

**Demo Request Benchmarks:**
- Demo Request Rate: 2-5% of emails sent
- Demo Show-Up Rate: 60-70% of booked demos
- Demo-to-Trial Conversion: 40-60%
- Trial-to-Paid Conversion: 15-25%

**Improve demo conversion by:**
- Qualifying harder before booking (ask screening questions)
- Sending calendar reminder 1 day before + 1 hour before
- Starting demo with "What's your biggest challenge with [process]?" not "Let me show you features"
- Following up within 2 hours after demo with trial access + next steps

---

## When to Offer Free Trial vs. Demo

### Offer Demo First If:
- Product is complex (>30 minutes to learn)
- Price point >$500/month (requires explanation)
- They need to see customization options
- Integration setup is non-trivial

### Offer Free Trial First If:
- Product is self-explanatory (clean UX)
- Price point <$100/month (low-risk)
- They have hands-on buying style (some industries prefer this)
- You have strong onboarding automation

**Hybrid Approach:**
Offer both in Email 1: "Want a 15-min demo, or prefer to try it yourself with a free trial?"
