# SaaS - Partnership Sequence

**Industry:** SaaS
**Use Case:** Strategic Partnership / Integration / Co-Marketing
**Sequence Length:** 5 emails over 30 days
**Best For:** Building partnerships with complementary SaaS companies

---

## Email 1: Partnership Opportunity Introduction (Day 0)

**Subject:** Partnership opportunity - [Your Company] + [Their Company]

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I'm [Your Name] from [Your Company] - we help [target audience] with [core value proposition].

I noticed [Their Company] serves a similar audience but solves [different problem]. I think there's a strong partnership opportunity here.

**Why this makes sense:**
Our customers are frequently asking about [problem their product solves]. When they ask, we currently recommend [Competitor A] or [Competitor B], but I'd prefer to send them to [Their Company] if we can work something out.

**Potential partnership angles:**
• Integration (we have [X] customers who'd use both tools)
• Co-marketing (joint webinar, case study, or content)
• Referral agreement (revenue share or reciprocal referrals)

Worth a 20-minute exploratory call next week?

[Your Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Research their existing partnerships (check their integrations page, partner directory)
- Mention specific overlap in customer base (industry, company size, use case)
- Be specific about partnership type (integration vs. co-marketing vs. affiliate)

---

## Email 2: Customer Overlap Data (Day 3-4)

**Subject:** Re: Partnership opportunity - customer overlap data

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Quick follow-up with some data that might make this more concrete.

We ran a survey of our customer base and found:
• [X]% are currently using [Their Product or Category]
• [Y]% told us they need [feature/capability their product provides]
• [Z]% are actively evaluating tools in your category

We have [total customer count] customers, which means there are roughly [calculated number] potential users who'd benefit from a [Your Product] + [Their Product] integration.

**From their side:**
Based on your [recent funding news / website traffic / social following], I'd estimate [reasonable guess] of your customers might have similar overlap with our tool.

**Proposed next step:**
15-minute call to explore if this is worth pursuing? I can share more detailed customer data under NDA if needed.

[Your Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Use real data if you have it (even anonymized survey results)
- Make reasonable estimates about their customer base
- Show you've done research (mention their funding, team size, customer count if public)

---

## Email 3: Example Integration / Partnership (Day 7)

**Subject:** How we built our [Similar Company] integration

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Wanted to share a quick case study of a partnership that worked really well for us - might give you ideas for how this could work.

**Partnership:** [Your Company] + [Similar Company Name]

**What we built:**
[Specific integration or co-marketing initiative - describe in 2-3 sentences]

**Results:**
• [Their Company] got [specific benefit - e.g., "120 new qualified leads from our joint webinar"]
• [Your Company] got [specific benefit - e.g., "35% of our customers now use both tools"]
• Customers got [specific benefit - e.g., "seamless workflow between both platforms"]

**Time investment:**
[Realistic estimate - e.g., "Two weeks of dev work on each side, plus joint marketing materials"]

I think we could do something similar with [Their Company] given [specific reason related to their product/audience].

Want to explore this on a quick call?

[Your Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Pick a partnership example similar to what you're proposing
- Be realistic about time/resource investment (don't undersell the work)
- Focus on benefits for them and their customers, not just you

## Email 4: Direct Question Break-Up (Day 10-12)

**Subject:** Wrong timing or wrong fit?

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I've reached out a few times about a potential partnership between [Your Company] and [Their Company]. Haven't heard back, which probably means:

**Option A:** Wrong timing - you're focused on other priorities
**Option B:** Wrong fit - you don't see the customer overlap
**Option C:** Already committed - you have exclusivity with [Competitor]
**Option D:** Just missed my emails - inbox chaos is real

If it's A, happy to circle back in [3/6] months when things settle.
If it's B, C, or D - just a quick reply either way helps me move on or continue the conversation.

[Your Name]

P.S. - If it's "not now but maybe later," throw this on your calendar for 6 months from now: [calendly link with 6-month future date]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Acknowledge realistic reasons they might not respond
- Give them easy outs or easy ins
- Break-up emails often work for partnerships because BD/partner managers are evaluating many opportunities

## Email 5: Ecosystem / Market Trend Angle (Day 21-30)

**Subject:** The [ecosystem trend] trend - opportunity for [Their Company]?

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I know we couldn't connect earlier, but wanted to share something I'm seeing in the [their ecosystem/market] space.

**Trend we're noticing:**
[Specific trend - e.g., "More SaaS companies are building native integrations vs. relying on Zapier" or "Joint GTM partnerships are outperforming traditional co-marketing by 3x"]

**Why it matters:**
[Specific implications for their company - 2-3 sentences about competitive landscape, customer expectations, or market shifts]

**What we're doing:**
We're actively building [type of partnerships] with companies like [Example 1], [Example 2], and [Example 3]. The results have been [specific outcome].

If [Their Company] is thinking about [related strategy], I'd be happy to share what's working for us.

[Your Name]

P.S. - We're selecting [number] strategic partners for [specific initiative] in [timeframe]. If you're interested in being part of that cohort, let me know by [date].

**Personalization Notes:**
- Reference real ecosystem trends (check industry reports, competitor announcements)
- Create FOMO with "selecting X partners" or "limited cohort"
- Make it educational first, pitch second

## Partnership Email Best Practices

### Research Before Outreach:
1. **Check their existing partnerships** - Look at integrations page, partner directory, press releases
2. **Identify decision maker** - Partnership leads, BD team, or sometimes CEO at smaller companies
3. **Find warm intro if possible** - Investors, advisors, mutual customers
4. **Understand their partnership model** - Do they have a formal program? Revenue share? Technical requirements?

### Timing Considerations:
- **Best months:** January-March, September-October (budget planning periods)
- **Avoid:** November-December (holiday slowdown), July-August (vacation season)
- **Quarterly planning cycles:** Many companies plan partnerships quarterly

## Types of SaaS Partnerships

### 1. Integration Partnerships
**What:** Technical integration between products
**Best for:** Complementary products with shared customers
**Time investment:** High (dev resources required)
**Value:** Sticky, high-value for customers

**Email Focus:** Technical feasibility, API documentation, customer demand

---

### 2. Co-Marketing Partnerships
**What:** Joint content, webinars, events, campaigns
**Best for:** Similar audiences, different solutions
**Time investment:** Medium (marketing resources)
**Value:** Lead generation, brand awareness

**Email Focus:** Audience overlap, content ideas, success metrics

---

### 3. Referral / Affiliate Partnerships
**What:** Revenue share for sending customers to each other
**Best for:** Complementary products, no integration needed
**Time investment:** Low (mostly agreement/tracking setup)
**Value:** New customer acquisition channel

**Email Focus:** Customer overlap data, commission structure, referral volume estimates

---

### 4. Reseller Partnerships
**What:** One company sells the other's product
**Best for:** Established sales channel + new product
**Time investment:** Medium (sales enablement, support)
**Value:** Revenue expansion, market access

**Email Focus:** Sales team size, target markets, commission/margin expectations

## Partnership Pitch Deck (Optional Attachment)

Consider creating a lightweight partnership deck (5-7 slides) to attach in Email 2:

1. **Slide 1:** Who we are + what we do
2. **Slide 2:** Our customer base (size, industries, roles)
3. **Slide 3:** Customer overlap evidence
4. **Slide 4:** Partnership opportunity options
5. **Slide 5:** Example partnerships + results
6. **Slide 6:** Proposed timeline + next steps

Keep it visual and data-driven. Avoid heavy text.

## Expected Results

**Partnership Email Benchmarks:**
- Response Rate: 10-20% (higher than sales emails because less volume)
- Meeting Booking Rate: 5-10%
- Partnership Close Rate: 20-40% of meetings → signed agreement
- Time to Close: 1-3 months on average

**Improve partnership outreach by:**
- Getting warm intros from investors, advisors, or mutual customers (5x response rate vs. cold)
- Targeting companies at similar stage (early-stage to early-stage, etc.)
- Leading with customer data, not product features
- Following up persistently but respectfully (partnerships take longer than sales deals)

---

## When to Pursue Partnerships

### Good Fit If:
- Clear customer overlap (>20% of your customers use their product or vice versa)
- Complementary solutions (not competitive)
- Similar company stage and values
- Both have resources to invest in partnership

### Bad Fit If:
- No customer overlap (partnership won't get used)
- Competitive products (conflict of interest)
- Mismatched company stage (enterprise vs. startup = different priorities)
- One-sided benefit (partnerships need mutual value)

## Post-Meeting: Partnership Agreement Checklist

If you get to a partnership conversation, clarify these points:

- [ ] **Goals:** What success looks like for both sides
- [ ] **Responsibilities:** Who builds/does what
- [ ] **Timeline:** Key milestones and deadlines
- [ ] **Resources:** Dev time, marketing budget, sales team involvement
- [ ] **Metrics:** How you'll measure success
- [ ] **Revenue model:** Commission, rev share, or non-monetary
- [ ] **Term length:** 6 months, 1 year, ongoing?
- [ ] **Exit clause:** How either party can end partnership
