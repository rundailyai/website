# Agency - Portfolio Review / Demo Request Sequence

**Industry:** Creative/Marketing/Development Agency
**Use Case:** Portfolio Review / Work Examples / Capabilities Demo
**Sequence Length:** 5 emails over 30 days
**Best For:** Getting prospects to review your work and consider hiring your agency

---

## Email 1: Portfolio Introduction with Relevant Work (Day 0)

**Subject:** [Company Name] - portfolio examples for [specific service]

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I run [Agency Name] - we specialize in [specific service] for [their industry] companies.

I've worked with brands like [Similar Client 1], [Similar Client 2], and [Similar Client 3] on projects ranging from [project type A] to [project type B].

**Recent work that might be relevant:**

**[Client Name]** - [Project Type]
[One sentence describing the challenge]
[One sentence describing what you built/created]
Result: [Specific metric or outcome]
[Link to case study or live work]

**[Client Name]** - [Project Type]
[One sentence describing the challenge]
[One sentence describing what you built/created]
Result: [Specific metric or outcome]
[Link to case study or live work]

Full portfolio: [link]

If [Company Name] is exploring [specific service type], I'd love to show you more examples and discuss how we might help.

[Your Name]
[Agency Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Pick 2-3 portfolio pieces that match their industry or challenge
- Link directly to live work when possible (websites, campaigns, designs)
- Use specific outcomes not vague "increased engagement"

## Portfolio Presentation Best Practices

### What to Include:
1. **Live links over screenshots** - Let them interact with the work
2. **Context over beauty shots** - Explain the problem, approach, and result
3. **Metrics over claims** - "Increased conversion 47%" vs "Beautiful design"
4. **Process over final product** - Show how you think, not just what you made
5. **Client testimonials** - Video testimonials > written quotes > no testimonials

### What to Exclude:
- Old work (nothing older than 2-3 years unless legendary)
- NDA'd work without client permission
- Failed projects or underwhelming results
- Work outside your current focus (confuses positioning)

## Portfolio Format Options

### Option 1: Traditional Portfolio Website
**Best for:** Design, development, branding agencies
**Tools:** Webflow, Behance, Dribbble, custom site
**Pros:** Professional, comprehensive, SEO benefits
**Cons:** Can be overwhelming, prospects may not explore fully

### Option 2: Personalized Portfolio PDF
**Best for:** Email outreach, specific prospect situations
**Tools:** Figma, Canva, InDesign
**Pros:** Curated, tailored to prospect, easy to share
**Cons:** Time-consuming to create per prospect

### Option 3: Video Portfolio Walkthrough
**Best for:** Complex projects, process-heavy work
**Tools:** Loom, Vidyard, Vimeo
**Pros:** Engaging, shows personality, explains thinking
**Cons:** Lower completion rate than static portfolio

### Option 4: Interactive Notion Portfolio
**Best for:** Strategy, content, consulting work
**Tools:** Notion, Coda, Airtable
**Pros:** Easy to update, client can filter by category/industry
**Cons:** Less visually impressive than designed portfolio

**Recommended:** Use combination - comprehensive website + customized PDF/video for outreach

## A/B Testing Variations

### Subject Line Tests:
- **Email 1 Options:**
  - "[Company Name] - [Service] portfolio examples"
  - "Work samples for [Industry] brands"
  - "Portfolio for [First Name] at [Company Name]"

- **Email 3 Options:**
  - "Before/After: [Client] transformation"
  - "Case study: [Specific outcome] in 90 days"
  - "[Similar Client] results - [Company Name] might find this interesting"

### Email Body Tests:
- **Short vs. Long:** Test 3 portfolio examples vs. 1 detailed example
- **Link placement:** Portfolio link at top vs. bottom
- **CTA style:** "Want to see more?" vs. "15-minute portfolio review?" vs. Direct calendly link

## Expected Results

**Portfolio-Focused Email Benchmarks:**
- Response Rate: 6-12% (visual work tends to get higher engagement)
- Portfolio Click Rate: 15-25% of opens
- Meeting Booking Rate: 2-5% of total emails sent
- Portfolio View → Meeting: 20-30%

**Improve results by:**
- Showing work that looks like theirs (same industry, aesthetic, format)
- Loading fast (optimize images, use CDN, avoid heavy sites)
- Mobile-optimized (50%+ will view on phone)
- Including results/outcomes, not just pretty pictures

## Qualifying Questions During Portfolio Review

When you get a meeting to review portfolio, ask:

1. **"What specific aspects of our work caught your attention?"**
   - Tells you what they value (design? results? process? industry expertise?)

2. **"What projects are you currently evaluating agencies for?"**
   - Qualifies if they're active buyer or just browsing

3. **"What's your timeline for making a decision?"**
   - Qualifies urgency and budget availability

4. **"What's worked or not worked with agencies you've hired before?"**
   - Reveals red flags and expectations

5. **"What's your budget range for this project?"**
   - Qualifies if they're realistic about cost

Don't just present portfolio - use it as qualification tool.

## Common Objections & Responses

### "Your work is great but too expensive"
**Response:** "I appreciate that. Most of our clients felt that way initially, but found the ROI made sense when they saw [specific outcome]. What budget range were you thinking, and I can show you how we might adjust scope to fit?"

### "We need someone with more experience in [niche]"
**Response:** "That's fair. We've worked in [related industries that transfer skills], and here's why that's actually an advantage: [explain fresh perspective, cross-industry innovation, etc.]. Would you be open to a pilot project to test the fit?"

### "We're doing this in-house"
**Response:** "That's great - in-house teams often benefit from external support during [specific scenarios: peak periods, specialized projects, fresh perspective]. We work alongside in-house teams as an extension. Worth exploring?"

### "Can you show us work exactly like [specific thing]?"
**Response:** "I don't have an exact match, but here's why: [explain custom approach, unique client situations, etc.]. The closest parallel is [portfolio piece], and here's how we'd adapt that thinking for [Company Name]..."

## Portfolio-Specific Follow-Up Cadence

After someone views your portfolio:

- **Within 2 hours:** Send thank you + next step proposal
- **Day 3:** Share related case study or blog post
- **Day 7:** Ask specific question about their project
- **Day 14:** Share new work or client testimonial
- **Day 30:** Final check-in with new angle

Track portfolio engagement (most portfolio platforms have analytics) and follow up based on what they viewed.
