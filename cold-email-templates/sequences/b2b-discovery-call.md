# B2B Services - Discovery Call / Consultation Sequence

**Industry:** B2B Professional Services
**Use Case:** Discovery Call / Free Consultation / Needs Assessment
**Sequence Length:** 5 emails over 30 days
**Best For:** Offering consultative assessment to demonstrate expertise

---

## Email 1: Free Assessment Offer (Day 0)

**Subject:** Complimentary [specific assessment] for [Company Name]

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I work with [their company type] companies to [specific outcome your service delivers].

Based on [specific observation about their company - growth, expansion, recent news], I'd guess [Company Name] is dealing with [predictable challenge for their situation].

**I'd like to offer a complimentary [specific assessment]:**

In a 30-minute call, I'll:
• Review your current [process/system/approach]
• Identify gaps or risks based on [industry best practices/regulations/benchmarks]
• Provide 3-5 actionable recommendations you can implement immediately

**No pitch, no obligation** - just a professional assessment from someone who's done this with [X] companies in [their industry].

Worth 30 minutes of your time?

[Your Name]
[Your Company]

P.S. - We recently did this for [Similar Company] and identified [specific finding] that would have cost them $[X] if unaddressed. Happy to share that case study on the call.

**Personalization Notes:**
- Be specific about what assessment covers (not vague "strategy session")
- Emphasize no-obligation nature (reduces friction)
- Mention specific finding from similar company (proves value)

## Discovery Call / Consultation Best Practices

### Structuring the Assessment Call:

**First 5 minutes: Context gathering**
- "Tell me about [Company Name] - what do you do, who do you serve?"
- "Walk me through your current [process/approach] for [area you help with]"
- "What prompted you to take this call?"

**Next 15 minutes: Deep dive questions**
- Ask diagnostic questions based on your framework
- Listen more than you talk (80/20 rule)
- Take notes (shows professionalism)

**Last 10 minutes: Initial findings + recommendations**
- "Here's what I'm hearing..."
- "Based on [X], here are 3 things I'd recommend..."
- "The top priority would be [Y] because [specific reason]"

**Final 5 minutes: Next steps (if applicable)**
- "Do these recommendations make sense?"
- "What would you like to tackle first?"
- "Would you like our help with any of this, or are you planning to handle it internally?"

## Offering Value Without Giving Away Methodology

### What to Share (builds trust):
- High-level recommendations
- What to prioritize and why
- Resources and frameworks
- Common mistakes to avoid

### What to Hold Back (protects IP):
- Proprietary methodologies
- Detailed implementation playbooks
- Specific templates/tools you've built
- Client-specific strategies you've developed

**The line:** Give them the "what" and "why" freely. Charge for the "how" and "doing it for them."

## Converting Free Consultations to Paid Engagements

### Natural Transition Language:

**Option 1: Direct ask**
"Based on what we discussed, I think we could help with [X]. Would you like me to put together a proposal?"

**Option 2: Tiered options**
"You have three paths: [DIY], [Done-with-you], or [Done-for-you]. Which fits best for [Company Name]'s bandwidth and timeline?"

**Option 3: Start small**
"What if we started with just [Phase 1 / specific deliverable] as a pilot? If that goes well, we can discuss [Phase 2]."

**Conversion rate target:** 30-50% of discovery calls should convert to paid proposals.

**If lower than 30%:** You're either:
- Targeting wrong prospects (poor qualification)
- Not demonstrating expertise effectively (weak assessment)
- Pricing is off-market (too high or too low)
- Not asking for the business (passive closing)

## When Free Consultations Work Best

### Ideal Scenarios:
- High-ticket services ($5K+ engagements)
- Complex/technical services that require education
- Long sales cycles where trust is critical
- Competitive markets where differentiation is hard
- Services where "trying before buying" isn't possible

### When to Skip Free Consultations:
- Low-ticket offerings (not worth your time)
- Productized services with clear pricing (consultation not needed)
- Inbound leads already educated (they know what they need)
- When you're overbooked (scarcity is better positioning)

---

## Preventing "Consulting for Free" Problem

### Red flags someone just wants free advice:

- They book multiple "follow-up" calls without moving forward
- They ask for detailed implementation plans during assessment
- They request written proposals with extensive detail before any commitment
- They're "just exploring" with no timeline or budget
- They're already working with a competitor (competitor research in disguise)

### Protect your time:

**Set boundaries upfront:**
"This 30-minute assessment will give you directional recommendations. Detailed implementation planning is part of our paid engagement."

**Qualify before booking:**
"Are you actively looking for support with [X], or more in exploratory mode?" (Don't book calls with pure explorers)

**Limit free consultation to 30 minutes:**
Be disciplined about time. If it goes over, say: "We're at 30 minutes - happy to continue this conversation as a paid engagement if you'd like to go deeper."

## Post-Assessment Follow-Up

### Same day as call:
Send summary email with:
- Thank you for their time
- Recap of 3-5 recommendations discussed
- Next step options (DIY, done-with-you, done-for-you)
- Calendar link if they want to continue conversation

### Day 3 if no response:
"Following up on our assessment - any questions about recommendations?"

### Day 7 if no response:
"Checking in - are you moving forward with any of the recommendations? Happy to help if you decide you want support."

### Day 14 if no response:
"Final check-in - should I send over a proposal for [specific recommendation] or are you handling this internally?"

**After 2 weeks:** Move on. Add them to nurture email list for future opportunities.

---

## Nurture Strategy for "Not Now" Prospects

If they liked the assessment but timing wasn't right:

- Add to quarterly newsletter (industry insights, not sales pitches)
- Connect on LinkedIn, engage with their content
- Tag them in CRM for 3-month follow-up
- Send relevant content when you publish something useful
- Invite to webinars or events (low-commitment touchpoints)

**Many assessments convert 3-12 months later** when timing aligns. Stay top-of-mind without being pushy.
