# Agency - Partnership Sequence

**Industry:** Creative/Marketing/Development Agency
**Use Case:** Strategic Partnership / White-Label / Referral Network
**Sequence Length:** 5 emails over 30 days
**Best For:** Building partnerships with complementary agencies or service providers

---

## Email 1: Partnership Opportunity Introduction (Day 0)

**Subject:** Partnership opportunity - [Your Agency] + [Their Agency]

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I run [Your Agency Name] - we specialize in [your core service] for [your target market].

I came across [Their Agency Name] and noticed you focus on [their core service]. I think there's a strong partnership opportunity here because our services complement each other without competing.

**Why this makes sense:**

Our clients frequently need [service they provide] after we deliver [service you provide]. For example, we just finished [project type] for [client type], and they asked for recommendations for [their service]. I'd rather refer them to a trusted partner than let them find someone random.

**Potential partnership models:**
• Referral network (send overflow/complementary work to each other)
• White-label partnership (you deliver under our brand or vice versa)
• Joint pitches (pursue larger projects together)

Worth a 20-minute conversation to explore?

[Your Name]
[Your Agency Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Research their client work (check portfolio, case studies, testimonials)
- Identify clear service complement (e.g., you do branding, they do web dev)
- Show you understand their positioning (boutique vs. full-service, industry focus, etc.)

## Email 2: Client Overlap Evidence (Day 3-4)

**Subject:** Re: Partnership opportunity - client overlap data

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Following up with some data that might make this more concrete.

**From our side:**
In the past 6 months, we've had [X] clients request [service they provide] after we completed [service you provide]. We don't offer that service, so we've been referring them to [current partner or "various agencies"].

**Potential volume:**
We typically work with [Y] new clients per quarter, and roughly [Z]% need [their service] within 3-6 months of our engagement.

**From your side:**
Based on your portfolio, it looks like you work with [similar client types]. I'd imagine some of your clients need [your service] before/after your work?

**Proposed partnership structure:**
• We refer clients who need [their service]
• You refer clients who need [our service]
• [Optional: Commission structure] [X]% referral fee or reciprocal value exchange
• Quarterly check-in to ensure quality and volume

15-minute call to discuss the details?

[Your Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Use real numbers if possible (be conservative if estimating)
- Propose specific partnership structure (shows you've thought it through)
- Address commission/value exchange upfront (avoids awkward conversation later)

## Email 4: Direct Question Break-Up (Day 10-12)

**Subject:** Not the right fit?

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I've reached out a few times about partnering between [Your Agency] and [Their Agency]. Haven't heard back, so I'm guessing:

**Option A:** You already have strong referral partners for this
**Option B:** You prefer to build out these services in-house
**Option C:** Your client volume doesn't support partnerships right now
**Option D:** Just bad timing - you're swamped

If it's A, B, or C - totally understand, and I'll take you off my list.

If it's D - should I follow up in a few months when things calm down?

Either way, appreciate you reading this far.

[Your Name]

P.S. - If you ever need [your specific service] for a client project, happy to help even if we don't formalize a partnership. Just reach out.

**Personalization Notes:**
- Give realistic reasons they might not respond (not judgmental)
- Offer help regardless (relationship building, not just transactional)
- Break-up emails work well for B2B partnerships (professionals respond to directness)

## Agency Partnership Best Practices

### Types of Agency Partnerships

#### 1. Referral Network
**Structure:** Send overflow or out-of-scope work to each other
**Best for:** Agencies with complementary services
**Commission:** 10-20% of project value or reciprocal value
**Example:** Branding agency refers web development work

#### 2. White-Label Partnership
**Structure:** One agency delivers work under another's brand
**Best for:** Agencies wanting to offer services without hiring specialists
**Payment:** Wholesale pricing (typically 50-70% of retail)
**Example:** Marketing agency white-labels video production

#### 3. Joint Venture
**Structure:** Co-pitch and co-deliver large projects
**Best for:** Complementary agencies pursuing bigger opportunities
**Split:** Based on scope of work (usually 50/50 or 60/40)
**Example:** Design agency + development agency co-pitch website redesign

#### 4. Affiliate/Reseller
**Structure:** One agency sells the other's productized service
**Best for:** Agencies with productized offerings (audits, templates, courses)
**Commission:** 20-30% of sale price
**Example:** SEO agency resells content agency's blog writing service

## Partnership Agreement Essentials

### Must-Have Terms:
- [ ] **Services exchanged** - Specific scope of what each party provides
- [ ] **Quality standards** - How work is reviewed and approved
- [ ] **Pricing/commission structure** - Clear financial terms
- [ ] **Payment terms** - Net 30, upfront, milestone-based, etc.
- [ ] **Client communication** - Who interfaces with end client
- [ ] **Liability** - Who's responsible if something goes wrong
- [ ] **Exclusivity** - Can you partner with their competitors?
- [ ] **Term and termination** - How long partnership lasts, exit terms
- [ ] **Confidentiality** - Client data, pricing, methods protection
- [ ] **Dispute resolution** - What happens if there's a conflict

**Start simple:** Use a 2-page partnership agreement for first few projects, formalize if it works.

## Partnership Pitch Deck (Email 2 Attachment)

Create lightweight partnership deck (5-7 slides):

1. **About Us** - Your agency overview, specialties, team size
2. **Why Partner** - Market opportunity, client demand evidence
3. **Partnership Models** - Referral, white-label, JV options
4. **Client Overlap** - Data showing potential volume
5. **Example Partners** - Success stories (anonymized if needed)
6. **Process** - How referrals/projects flow between agencies
7. **Next Steps** - Call to action, timeline, contact info

Keep it visual and data-driven. Share via Google Slides or PDF.

## Red Flags to Watch For

**Avoid partnerships with agencies that:**
- Have poor reputation in the market (ask around, check reviews)
- Miss deadlines or communicate poorly during discussions (won't get better)
- Want exclusive partnerships before proving mutual value
- Can't provide client references or portfolio examples
- Significantly undercut market rates (quality concern)
- Have high team turnover (instability warning sign)

**Your reputation is on the line** when you refer clients - partner carefully.

## Expected Results

**Agency Partnership Email Benchmarks:**
- Response Rate: 15-25% (higher than client outreach)
- Meeting Booking Rate: 8-15%
- Meeting → Trial Partnership: 40-60%
- Trial → Ongoing Partnership: 30-50%

**Timeline:** 2-4 months from first email to active partnership (includes trial period)

**Improve results by:**
- Getting warm intro from mutual client or industry connection (3x response rate)
- Targeting agencies in different geographic markets (less threatening)
- Starting with small trial referral (proves value before formal agreement)
- Over-communicating during first few referrals (builds trust)

## Partnership Maintenance

Once partnership is established:

### Monthly:
- Share referral pipeline updates
- Flag any client issues or feedback
- Discuss potential joint opportunities

### Quarterly:
- Review volume and revenue exchanged
- Assess client satisfaction
- Identify improvement opportunities
- Discuss expanding partnership scope

### Annually:
- Formal partnership review meeting
- Renew or revise partnership terms
- Set goals for coming year
- Consider co-marketing initiatives

**Most partnerships fail due to lack of communication** - over-communicate, especially early on.

---

## Scaling Agency Partnerships

### Start: 1-2 partners
**Focus:** Proving the model, refining process, building trust

### Growth: 3-5 partners
**Focus:** Systematizing referrals, standardizing quality checks

### Scale: 5+ partners
**Focus:** Partner portal, automated workflows, tiered partnership levels

**Don't scale too fast** - better to have 3 excellent partners than 10 mediocre ones.

---

## Partnership ROI Calculation

**Track these metrics:**
- Referrals sent (volume and value)
- Referrals received (volume and value)
- Client satisfaction scores for partnership work
- Revenue from partnership vs. direct sales
- Time saved by not building in-house capability

**Healthy partnership indicators:**
- Roughly balanced value exchange (within 70/30 over 12 months)
- Client retention after partnership work (should be same or higher)
- Repeat referrals (partners keep sending work = they trust you)
- Expansion opportunities (joint pitches, co-marketing)

If partnership is one-sided after 6 months, address it or end it.
