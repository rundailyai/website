# SaaS - Meeting Booking Sequence

**Industry:** SaaS
**Use Case:** Meeting Booking / Discovery Call
**Sequence Length:** 5 emails over 30 days
**Best For:** Booking meetings with potential customers to discuss their challenges

---

## Email 1: Initial Outreach (Day 0)

**Subject:** Quick question about [Company Name]'s [specific process]

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I noticed [Company Name] is [specific observation from their website/LinkedIn - e.g., "expanding into the European market" or "recently raised Series B funding"].

Most [their role/industry] teams I work with struggle with [specific pain point related to your solution]. For example, one VP of Sales I spoke with was spending 15+ hours weekly on [manual task your product automates].

We built [Your Product] specifically to help [their company type] teams [achieve specific outcome] without [common drawback].

Worth a 15-minute conversation?

[Your Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Replace [specific observation] with something from their recent LinkedIn posts, company blog, or press releases
- Use [their role/industry] that matches their actual position
- Reference [specific pain point] that's relevant to their company stage/size

---

## Email 2: Value-Add Follow-Up (Day 3-4)

**Subject:** Re: Quick question about [Company Name]'s [specific process]

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I know inboxes get busy. Wanted to share something that might be useful regardless of whether we talk.

We recently published a guide on [relevant topic to their pain point] - specifically for [their industry/role]. It covers:

• [Specific tactic #1]
• [Specific tactic #2]
• [Specific tactic #3]

Here's the link: [actual resource link]

Also happy to jump on a quick call if you're exploring solutions for [their pain point] - we've helped [similar company type] companies like [Name Drop 1] and [Name Drop 2] reduce [metric] by [percentage/number].

Best time for a 15-min chat next week?

[Your Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Provide actual value (guide, template, checklist) - not gated content
- Name drop companies similar in size/industry to theirs
- Use specific metrics (not vague "improve efficiency")

---

## Email 3: Social Proof / Case Study (Day 7)

**Subject:** How [Similar Company] solved [specific problem]

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Following up one more time because I recently helped [Similar Company Name] tackle the exact challenge I mentioned.

**Their situation:**
They were [specific problem context] which caused [negative outcome - time lost, revenue impact, team frustration].

**What we did:**
Implemented [Your Product]'s [specific feature] to [specific action].

**Result:**
- [Metric improvement #1 with specific number/percentage]
- [Metric improvement #2 with specific number/percentage]
- [Qualitative benefit - e.g., "Their SDR team cut prospecting time from 3 hours to 45 minutes daily"]

Full case study here: [link]

Since [Company Name] is also [relevant similarity to case study company], I thought this might resonate.

15 minutes to explore if this could work for your team?

[Your Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Use a case study from their specific industry or company stage
- Focus on outcomes they'd care about (revenue, time saved, team efficiency)
- Draw explicit parallel between case study company and their situation

---

## Email 4: Break-Up Email (Day 10-12)

**Subject:** Should I close your file?

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I've reached out a few times about [specific value proposition] but haven't heard back - which usually means:

1. Not a priority right now
2. Wrong timing
3. You're handling [pain point] a different way
4. My emails aren't relevant

If it's #1 or #2, no problem at all - should I follow up in [3/6] months instead?

If it's #3 or #4, I'll close your file and won't bother you again.

Either way, appreciate you reading this far.

[Your Name]

P.S. - If there *is* interest but you're just swamped, this Calendly link makes it easy to grab time without the back-and-forth: [calendly link]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Be direct and respectful of their time
- Give them an easy out or an easy in
- The P.S. often gets the most responses in break-up emails

---

## Email 5: Soft Re-Engagement (Day 21-30)

**Subject:** [Industry] trend I thought you'd find interesting

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I know we couldn't connect earlier, but wanted to flag something I'm seeing across [their industry] companies right now.

We're noticing that [specific trend observation] - for example:
- [Specific data point or industry shift]
- [How it's impacting companies like theirs]
- [Strategic implication]

We wrote up our analysis here: [link to blog post/resource]

[Optional: Soft pitch paragraph]
We're helping [their industry] teams adapt to this by [brief solution description]. Happy to share what's working if you're thinking about this challenge.

Hope this is useful either way.

[Your Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Lead with genuine insight or value, not a pitch
- Reference recent industry news, regulatory changes, or market shifts
- Make the pitch optional/soft - position yourself as a resource, not a salesperson

## Sequence Timing Summary

| Email | Day | Send Time (Recommended) | Open Rate Benchmark |
|-------|-----|------------------------|---------------------|
| Email 1 | Day 0 | Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10am local time | 40-50% |
| Email 2 | Day 3-4 | 24-72 hours after Email 1 | 30-40% |
| Email 3 | Day 7 | One week after Email 1 | 25-35% |
| Email 4 | Day 10-12 | 10-12 days after Email 1 | 35-45% (break-up emails perform well) |
| Email 5 | Day 21-30 | 3-4 weeks after Email 1 | 20-30% |

## A/B Testing Variations

### Subject Line Alternatives for Email 1:
- "[Company Name] + [Your Company] - worth exploring?"
- "15 minutes to discuss [specific outcome]?"
- "Saw your post about [topic] - quick idea"

### Opening Line Alternatives for Email 1:
- "I've been following [Company Name]'s growth in [market/vertical]..."
- "Your recent post about [topic] resonated - we work with a lot of [their role] leaders facing [similar challenge]"
- "Noticed you're hiring for [role] - usually a sign that [pain point] is becoming a priority"

---

## When to Pause or Stop Sequence

**Pause if they:**
- Open email multiple times but don't respond (they're interested but busy)
- Reply asking to "circle back in [timeframe]" (set calendar reminder)
- Engage with your content on LinkedIn/Twitter (warm them up via social first)

**Stop if they:**
- Reply with "not interested" (respect it)
- Mark as spam (unsubscribe them immediately)
- Have job title/role that's clearly wrong fit (research failure)

---

## Expected Results

**Industry Benchmarks for SaaS Cold Email:**
- Response Rate: 5-10% (good), 10-15% (excellent)
- Meeting Booking Rate: 1-3% of total emails sent
- Positive Response Rate: 3-7%

**To improve results:**
- Personalize first line of Email 1 for every prospect
- Send fewer, better-targeted emails vs. high volume
- Test different case studies in Email 3
- Track which email in sequence gets most replies (usually Email 1 or Email 4)
