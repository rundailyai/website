# Agency - Meeting Booking Sequence

**Industry:** Creative/Marketing/Development Agency
**Use Case:** Meeting Booking / Initial Consultation
**Sequence Length:** 5 emails over 30 days
**Best For:** Booking discovery calls with potential agency clients

---

## Email 1: Specific Problem Observation (Day 0)

**Subject:** [Company Name]'s [specific asset] caught my attention

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I was researching [their industry] companies and came across [specific thing - website, campaign, product launch, LinkedIn presence].

One thing jumped out: [specific observation that hints at problem your agency solves - e.g., "your website traffic looks strong (ranked for [keyword]) but I noticed your blog hasn't been updated since [date]" or "your Instagram engagement is solid but your website conversion path seems unclear"].

We work with [similar company type] companies to [specific outcome - e.g., "turn traffic into qualified leads" or "scale paid social without inflating CAC"].

Worth a 20-minute conversation to see if we can help with [specific goal]?

[Your Name]
[Your Agency Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Reference something specific from their website, social media, or recent marketing
- Point out gap/opportunity (not rudely, but helpfully)
- Match their company type (e-commerce vs. B2B SaaS vs. local service business)

---

## Email 2: Quick Audit / Value Add (Day 3-4)

**Subject:** Re: [Company Name]'s [specific asset] - quick audit

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Following up because I spent 15 minutes doing a quick audit of [Company Name]'s [website/social/ads/SEO].

**Quick findings:**
• [Specific observation #1 with data - e.g., "Your site speed is 4.2s on mobile (industry avg is 2.8s)"]
• [Specific observation #2 with opportunity - e.g., "You're ranking #8 for '[keyword]' - could probably hit top 3 with content refresh"]
• [Specific observation #3 with competitive insight - e.g., "[Competitor Name] is outspending you on [platform] by ~3x"]

Not a full audit obviously, but I put together a 2-page brief with recommendations: [link to Google Doc or PDF]

**No strings attached** - whether we work together or not, these are actionable.

If any of this resonates and you want to talk through strategy, happy to jump on a call next week.

[Your Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Actually do a quick audit (use free tools: PageSpeed Insights, Ahrefs free, SimilarWeb)
- Provide real value upfront (not gated behind a form)
- Focus on fixable issues with clear ROI

## Email 3: Case Study / Portfolio Example (Day 7)

**Subject:** How we helped [Similar Client] with [specific challenge]

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

Quick case study that's relevant to [Company Name]'s situation:

**Client:** [Similar Client Name - same industry/size as prospect]
**Challenge:** [Specific problem - 2 sentences]

**What we did:**
[Specific tactics - 3-4 bullet points with enough detail to show expertise]

**Results in [timeframe]:**
• [Metric #1 with before/after - e.g., "Organic traffic: 12K → 47K monthly visitors"]
• [Metric #2 with business impact - e.g., "Lead quality: 3.2% → 8.7% SQL rate"]
• [Metric #3 with ROI - e.g., "Customer acquisition cost down 42%"]

Full case study + portfolio: [link]

Since [Company Name] is also [relevant similarity - e.g., "a Series A SaaS company scaling from $2M to $10M ARR"], I thought this might be relevant.

15 minutes to explore if we could do something similar for your team?

[Your Name]

**Personalization Notes:**
- Pick case study that matches their industry, company stage, or challenge
- Use specific numbers (not vague "increased traffic")
- Link full portfolio with multiple examples (shows you're not a one-trick pony)

## Email 4: Direct Question Break-Up (Day 10-12)

**Subject:** Should I take [Company Name] off my list?

**Body:**

Hi [First Name],

I've reached out a few times about [specific value proposition - e.g., "helping scale your content marketing" or "optimizing your paid acquisition"].

Haven't heard back, so I'm assuming:

1. **Not a priority** - you're focused on other growth levers right now
2. **In-house already** - you've got this handled with your team
3. **Wrong timing** - budget/bandwidth constraints
4. **Working with another agency** - already have a partner for this

If it's #1 or #3, no worries - want me to check back in [3/6] months?

If it's #2 or #4, I'll take you off my list and won't bother you again.

[Your Name]

P.S. - If you're just slammed and there *is* interest, here's my calendar: [calendly link]. I'll keep next Tuesday and Thursday open for [Company Name].

**Personalization Notes:**
- Be direct but friendly (agencies benefit from relationship-building tone)
- Give them easy out or easy in
- The P.S. "keeping time open for you" adds exclusivity/urgency

**Why it matters for [Company Name]:**
[2-3 sentences on strategic implications for their specific situation]

**What top performers are doing:**
• [Specific tactic #1]
• [Specific tactic #2]
• [Specific tactic #3]

## Agency Outreach Best Practices

### Research Time Investment:
- **Minimum:** 10-15 minutes per prospect
- **Sweet spot:** 20-30 minutes for high-value targets
- **What to research:** Their current marketing (website, social, ads), recent funding/growth news, team size, likely budget range

### Audit Quality Matters:
Don't just say "I looked at your site" - provide:
- **Specific data points** (load time, ranking positions, engagement rate)
- **Competitive benchmarks** (vs. industry average or vs. specific competitor)
- **Actionable recommendations** (not just "improve SEO" but "target these 5 keywords")

---

## When to Discount/Incentivize

### Email 2 or 3: Limited-Time Audit Offer
"For the next [X] companies that book a call this month, we're including a full [specific audit type] audit (normally $[price], free as part of discovery)"

### Email 5: Seasonal Hook
"We have 2 client slots opening up in [upcoming month] - typically we have a waitlist but reaching out to prospects we've talked to before first"

## A/B Testing Variations

### Subject Line Tests:
- **Email 1 Options:**
  - "Question about [Company Name]'s [specific asset]"
  - "Noticed [specific observation] - idea to share"
  - "[Company Name] + [Agency Name] - fit worth exploring?"

- **Email 3 Options:**
  - "Case study: [Client] results - [specific metric]"
  - "How [Client] achieved [specific outcome]"
  - "[Similar Client] case study for [Company Name]"

### Opening Line Tests:
- "I've been following [Company Name]'s growth..."
- "Came across [specific thing] and had a thought..."
- "Your [specific asset] is impressive, but I noticed [opportunity]..."

## Expected Results

**Agency Cold Email Benchmarks:**
- Response Rate: 8-15% (higher than product sales due to relationship nature)
- Meeting Booking Rate: 3-7%
- Meeting Show-Up Rate: 70-80%
- Meeting → Proposal Rate: 40-60%
- Proposal → Client Rate: 20-35%

**Overall Cold Email → New Client:** 0.5-2%

**Improve results by:**
- Targeting companies at right budget tier (don't pitch enterprise services to startups)
- Sending from founder/partner, not generic agency email (builds trust)
- Including portfolio examples in every email after Email 1
- Following up with actual leads on LinkedIn (multi-channel approach)

---

## Ideal Client Profile for Agency Outreach

### Higher Response Rates:
- **Company stage:** Series A-C SaaS, $5M-50M revenue e-commerce, established B2B service companies
- **Decision maker:** VP Marketing, Head of Growth, CEO at smaller companies
- **Timing signals:** Recent funding, job postings for marketing roles, new product launch, rebrand

### Lower Response Rates:
- Pre-seed startups (budget constraints)
- Enterprise (complex procurement, long sales cycles)
- Companies with 10+ person in-house marketing teams (less need for agency)
- Companies that just hired CMO or Head of Marketing (want to prove themselves first)

## Post-Meeting: Discovery Call Checklist

Once you book a meeting, structure discovery as:

1. **Their goals (10 min):**
   - What are you trying to achieve in the next 6-12 months?
   - What's working well? What's not?
   - What have you tried before?

2. **Current state audit (5 min):**
   - Walk through what you've found in your research
   - Ask clarifying questions about budget, timeline, internal resources

3. **Your approach (3 min):**
   - Here's how we'd approach this...
   - Similar client examples
   - Rough timeline and investment range

4. **Next steps (2 min):**
   - "Does this direction make sense?"
   - If yes: "I'll send over a detailed proposal by [date]"
   - If no/not sure: "What concerns do you have?"

---

## Red Flags to Watch For

**Don't pursue if:**
- They want results "in a few weeks" (unrealistic expectations)
- Budget is far below market rate (will be high-maintenance, low-value client)
- They've burned through 3+ agencies in past year (problem client)
- They want to pay by results only (risk mismatch - early-stage exception)
- Decision-maker changes multiple times during outreach (internal chaos)

**Your time is valuable** - qualify hard, walk away from bad-fit prospects.
